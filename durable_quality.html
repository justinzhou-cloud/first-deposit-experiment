Durable User Quality: Treatment vs Control

Are incentive-created durables the same quality as organic durables?
DRI: Justin Zhou  •  Data pulled: Apr 29, 2026  •  All metrics measured 28 days post-bonus window
Key finding
Two different stories: Exp 1 durables are equal quality; Exp 2 durables are slightly lower quality.

Exp 1 (win 2nd deposit): treatment durables match or exceed control on every metric — retention is actually better (+2.8 pp). Exp 2 (win 1st deposit): treatment durables are modestly lower quality — retention is −2.1 pp, deposits −10%, POS −3.7%, dashing −1.2%. The first-deposit incentive creates real but somewhat shallower durables. The 2× cost advantage still holds after discounting for quality.

3 Interpretation

Two different stories

Exp 1 (2nd deposit): Treatment durables are indistinguishable from organic ones. Retention is actually +2.8 pp better, POS spend converges post-bonus, dashing is +3%. The $15 bonus reinforced behavior in users who were already on a deposit trajectory — it didn't attract low-quality users.

Exp 2 (1st deposit): Treatment durables are modestly lower quality. Retention is −2.1 pp, deposits are −10%, POS spend −3.7%, dashing −1.2%. The $20 bonus activated users with latent intent who had never deposited before — they're real dashers (27.3 hrs/month, $773 POS spend) but they sit at the lower end of the durable distribution.

Does this change the first-deposit recommendation?

No, but it nuances it. The quality discount in Exp 2 is ~5–10% across metrics. If we discount the $141 cost-per-incremental-durable by the retention gap (−2.1 pp / 34.8 pp baseline ≈ 6% lower quality), the effective cost is closer to ~$150. That's still well under Exp 1's $284. The 2× advantage holds after quality adjustment.

This is worth monitoring in your "$10 first purchase on us" experiment — if your bundling mechanism pulls in higher-quality marginal users (because they're spending on the card, not just depositing), you could narrow the quality gap while keeping the cost advantage.

Why the quality difference exists

The structural explanation is simple: Exp 1 reinforces; Exp 2 activates.

  • Exp 1 users already deposited once in December. The bonus nudges them to do it again. These are users with proven deposit behavior — the marginal ones are close to the median, hence similar quality.
  • Exp 2 users had never deposited. The bonus creates entirely new behavior. The marginal ones are at the very bottom of the "latent intent" distribution — they'll dash and spend, but less intensely than users who would have deposited without the nudge.

Implication for your experiment

Your "$10 first purchase on us" targets the same population as Exp 2 (never-deposited) but with a bundled deposit+spend mechanism. If the spend requirement filters for users with immediate purchase intent (not speculative depositors), the quality of marginal durables could be higher than Exp 2's. The 28-day post-bonus quality check should be built into your readout plan.

Exp 2 quality query (run this to fill TBD above) 
WITH payout_dasher AS (
  SELECT pa.id AS payout_account_id, dd.dasher_id
  FROM payout_service.public.payout_accounts pa
  JOIN proddb.public.dimension_dasher dd
    ON dd.payment_account_id = pa.payment_account_id
  WHERE dd.dasher_id IS NOT NULL
),

-- In-window durables only
tw_anc AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id, SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS amt
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-02-12'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <= DATE '2026-03-06'
  GROUP BY 1
  HAVING amt >= 200
),

durables AS (
  SELECT c.dasher_id, c.exp_group
  FROM proddb.nancyyang.win_first_deposit_0212_static c
  JOIN tw_anc t ON c.dasher_id = t.dasher_id
),

-- 28d post (Mar 7 – Apr 3)
post_anc AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id,
    SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS amt,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CAST(t.extract_datetime2 AS DATE)) AS dep_days
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  JOIN durables d ON pd.dasher_id = d.dasher_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-03-07'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <= DATE '2026-04-03'
  GROUP BY 1
),

-- 28-56d post (Apr 4 – Apr 30)
post2_anc AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id, SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS amt
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  JOIN durables d ON pd.dasher_id = d.dasher_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-04-04'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <= DATE '2026-04-30'
  GROUP BY 1
),

post_pos AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id,
    SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS amt,
    COUNT(*) AS n
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  JOIN durables d ON pd.dasher_id = d.dasher_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type = 'OUT_POS'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-03-07'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <= DATE '2026-04-03'
  GROUP BY 1
),

post_dash AS (
  SELECT ds.dasher_id, SUM(ds.total_active_time_seconds) / 3600.0 AS hrs
  FROM edw.dasher.dasher_shifts ds
  JOIN durables d ON ds.dasher_id = d.dasher_id
  WHERE ds.active_date BETWEEN DATE '2026-03-07' AND DATE '2026-04-03'
  GROUP BY 1
)

SELECT
  d.exp_group,
  COUNT(*) AS durables,
  -- Retention
  100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN COALESCE(pa.amt, 0) >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
    / COUNT(*) AS pct_dur_28d_post,
  100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN COALESCE(p2.amt, 0) >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
    / COUNT(*) AS pct_dur_28_56d_post,
  100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN COALESCE(pa.amt, 0) >= 200
              AND COALESCE(p2.amt, 0) >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)
    / COUNT(*) AS pct_dur_both,
  -- Deposits
  AVG(COALESCE(pa.amt, 0)) AS avg_post28_anc,
  AVG(COALESCE(pa.dep_days, 0)) AS avg_post28_dep_days,
  -- POS
  AVG(COALESCE(pp.amt, 0)) AS avg_post28_pos,
  AVG(COALESCE(pp.n, 0)) AS avg_post28_pos_n,
  -- Dashing
  AVG(COALESCE(pd2.hrs, 0)) AS avg_post28_hrs
FROM durables d
LEFT JOIN post_anc pa ON d.dasher_id = pa.dasher_id
LEFT JOIN post2_anc p2 ON d.dasher_id = p2.dasher_id
LEFT JOIN post_pos pp ON d.dasher_id = pp.dasher_id
LEFT JOIN post_dash pd2 ON d.dasher_id = pd2.dasher_id
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1;
