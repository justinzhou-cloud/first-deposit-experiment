2 Methodology: in-window incremental durability

Primary metric: % of users who deposited $200+ during the bonus eligibility window. Cost = reward × treatment qualifiers; incremental = treatment − (control rate × treatment size).

Why this is the right primary metric:

  • Window alignment is automatic. Cost numerator (bonuses paid) and outcome denominator (durables produced) are measured over the same period. No asymmetry, no inflation, no "free tail" where users become durable post-bonus without triggering payment.
  • It's exactly what the brief specified. Both experiment briefs framed success as "% of users who deposit $200+ in the target month." This methodology measures that directly — nothing more, nothing less.
  • Apples-to-apples within each experiment. Cost and lift are anchored to the same population behavior over the same window.

Why Nancy's "quirk" concern doesn't apply here: the original analysis used a 28-day rolling durability window that extended past Exp 2's 22-day bonus eligibility. Users could become "durable" in the 6-day tail without triggering payment — counted in lift, missing from cost. This methodology forces both metrics onto the same window, eliminating that asymmetry.

Caveats worth flagging:

  • Different window lengths. Exp 1 used 31 days (Jan), Exp 2 used 22 days (Feb 12–Mar 6). Exp 2's $200 bar is ~40% harder to clear per unit time, which actually strengthens the case for first-deposit.
  • "$200 in window" ≠ behavioral durability. A user could hit $200 once and never deposit again. The metric is a leading indicator. We pair it with a sustainability check (Section 3).
  • For evergreen scaling, this metric is appropriate. An always-on $20-for-$200-in-rolling-period offer would have cost = $20 × monthly qualifiers, which is exactly what we're measuring.

4 So where should I focus — first or second deposits?

First deposit, with high confidence

On the in-window primary metric, first-deposit intervention beats second-deposit by 2× ($141 vs $284 per incremental durable). The 28-day post-bonus sustainability check confirms it's not pure incentive-chasing — both experiments retain a meaningful share of their lift after the bonus expires. Structural reasons for the first-deposit advantage:

  • Larger addressable pool: ~1.7M users have never deposited (Exp 2 cohort) vs. ~67K first-time depositors per month (Exp 1 cohort). 25× more users to lift.
  • More elastic population: Exp 2's cohort had latent intent that wouldn't otherwise convert. Exp 1's cohort was already on a deposit trajectory; the bonus mostly accelerated something that would have happened anyway, hence the small +5.6% in-window lift.
  • Lift survives post-promo: Exp 2's effect retained 56% in the 28-day post window (+9.24% relative); Exp 1 retained 70% of its smaller lift (+3.95% relative). Both real, but Exp 2's larger absolute lift translates to many more incremental durables.

Why I'd still consider a small recurring-deposit play

Exp 1 had one underrated property: low absolute spend ($35K total). If you can find a higher-elasticity sub-segment of first-time depositors (e.g., those with strong dashing income or active P2P inflows), the per-user economics could improve. But this is a smaller program, not a primary growth lever.

Concrete answer for your $10-on-us experiment

  • You're already targeting first-deposit. That's the right funnel position.
  • Your $10 reward is half of Exp 2's $20. If your bundling mechanism preserves Exp 2's ~16.5% relative lift, the per-conversion cost halves and you'd land near $70/incremental qualifier — meaningfully under Nancy's $141. The risk is a smaller reward producing a smaller lift; only the experiment will tell.
  • Your bundling mechanism is the unique value-add. Don't optimize for "where to intervene" — settled. Optimize for "does the deposit+spend bundle outperform a flat deposit bonus?"
  • Pre-register the metric explicitly: % of users who deposited $10+ AND made a card purchase within May 1–15, with cost per incremental qualifier as the primary success metric. Add 28-day-post-bonus durability (Jun) as the sustainability check.

9 SQL queries (for QC)
Exp 1 results: Jan/Feb/Mar durability by exp_group 
WITH payout_dasher AS (
  SELECT pa.id AS payout_account_id, dd.dasher_id
  FROM payout_service.public.payout_accounts pa
  JOIN proddb.public.dimension_dasher dd
    ON dd.payment_account_id = pa.payment_account_id
  WHERE dd.dasher_id IS NOT NULL
),
monthly_anc AS (
  SELECT
    pd.dasher_id,
    DATE_TRUNC('month', CAST(t.extract_datetime2 AS DATE)) AS month,
    SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS anc,
    COUNT(*) AS n_dep
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-01-01'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <  DATE '2026-04-01'
  GROUP BY 1, 2
),
per_user AS (
  SELECT
    c.dasher_id,
    c.exp_group,
    COALESCE(MAX(CASE WHEN m.month = DATE '2026-01-01' THEN m.anc END), 0)   AS jan_anc,
    COALESCE(MAX(CASE WHEN m.month = DATE '2026-01-01' THEN m.n_dep END), 0) AS jan_n_dep,
    COALESCE(MAX(CASE WHEN m.month = DATE '2026-02-01' THEN m.anc END), 0)   AS feb_anc,
    COALESCE(MAX(CASE WHEN m.month = DATE '2026-03-01' THEN m.anc END), 0)   AS mar_anc
  FROM proddb.nancyyang.win_recurring_deposits_0120_static c
  LEFT JOIN monthly_anc m ON c.dasher_id = m.dasher_id
  GROUP BY 1, 2
)
SELECT
  exp_group,
  COUNT(*)                                                             AS users,
  SUM(CASE WHEN jan_n_dep >= 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                      AS any_jan_dep,
  SUM(CASE WHEN jan_anc   >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                    AS durable_jan,
  SUM(CASE WHEN feb_anc   >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                    AS durable_feb,
  SUM(CASE WHEN mar_anc   >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                    AS durable_mar,
  AVG(jan_anc)                                                          AS avg_jan_anc
FROM per_user
GROUP BY 1 ORDER BY 1;

WITH payout_dasher AS (
  SELECT pa.id AS payout_account_id, dd.dasher_id
  FROM payout_service.public.payout_accounts pa
  JOIN proddb.public.dimension_dasher dd
    ON dd.payment_account_id = pa.payment_account_id
  WHERE dd.dasher_id IS NOT NULL
),
anc AS (
  SELECT
    pd.dasher_id,
    CAST(t.extract_datetime2 AS DATE) AS d,
    ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction) AS amt
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-02-12'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <  DATE '2026-05-01'
),
per_user AS (
  SELECT
    c.dasher_id,
    c.exp_group,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN a.d BETWEEN DATE '2026-02-12' AND DATE '2026-03-06'
                      THEN a.amt END), 0) AS target_window_anc,
    COUNT(CASE   WHEN a.d BETWEEN DATE '2026-02-12' AND DATE '2026-03-06'
                 THEN 1 END)              AS target_window_dep,
    COALESCE(SUM(CASE WHEN a.d BETWEEN DATE '2026-04-01' AND DATE '2026-04-30'
                      THEN a.amt END), 0) AS apr_anc
  FROM proddb.nancyyang.win_first_deposit_0212_static c
  LEFT JOIN anc a ON c.dasher_id = a.dasher_id
  GROUP BY 1, 2
)
SELECT
  exp_group,
  COUNT(*)                                                                 AS users,
  SUM(CASE WHEN target_window_dep >= 1   THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                AS any_dep_target,
  SUM(CASE WHEN target_window_anc >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                AS durable_target,
  SUM(CASE WHEN apr_anc           >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                AS durable_apr,
  AVG(target_window_anc)                                                    AS avg_target_anc
FROM per_user
GROUP BY 1 ORDER BY 1;

WITH payout_dasher AS (
  SELECT pa.id AS payout_account_id, dd.dasher_id
  FROM payout_service.public.payout_accounts pa
  JOIN proddb.public.dimension_dasher dd
    ON dd.payment_account_id = pa.payment_account_id
  WHERE dd.dasher_id IS NOT NULL
),
jan_anc AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id, SUM(ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction)) AS amt
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 >= DATE '2026-01-01'
    AND t.extract_datetime2 <  DATE '2026-02-01'
  GROUP BY 1
),
labelled AS (
  SELECT c.dasher_id, c.exp_group,
    CASE
      WHEN c.dec_outside_gig >= GREATEST(c.dec_outside_p2p, c.dec_outside_ach, c.dec_outside_other)
           AND c.dec_outside_gig > 0  THEN 'GIG'
      WHEN c.dec_outside_p2p >= GREATEST(c.dec_outside_gig, c.dec_outside_ach, c.dec_outside_other)
           AND c.dec_outside_p2p > 0  THEN 'P2P'
      WHEN c.dec_outside_ach >= GREATEST(c.dec_outside_gig, c.dec_outside_p2p, c.dec_outside_other)
           AND c.dec_outside_ach > 0  THEN 'ACH'
      ELSE 'OTHER/MIXED'
    END AS dec_channel,
    COALESCE(j.amt, 0) AS jan_anc
  FROM proddb.nancyyang.win_recurring_deposits_0120_static c
  LEFT JOIN jan_anc j ON c.dasher_id = j.dasher_id
)
SELECT dec_channel, exp_group, COUNT(*) AS users,
  SUM(CASE WHEN jan_anc >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS durable_jan
FROM labelled
GROUP BY 1, 2 ORDER BY 1, 2;
Population funnel (Crimson joiners Oct '25–Mar '26) 
WITH payout_dasher AS (
  SELECT pa.id AS payout_account_id, dd.dasher_id
  FROM payout_service.public.payout_accounts pa
  JOIN proddb.public.dimension_dasher dd
    ON dd.payment_account_id = pa.payment_account_id
  WHERE dd.dasher_id IS NOT NULL
),
crimson_joiners AS (
  SELECT pd.dasher_id, MIN(CAST(t.extract_datetime2 AS TIMESTAMP_NTZ)) AS join_ts
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.extract_datetime2 IS NOT NULL
  GROUP BY 1
),
joiners_in_window AS (
  SELECT * FROM crimson_joiners
  WHERE CAST(join_ts AS DATE) BETWEEN DATE '2025-10-01' AND DATE '2026-03-31'
),
ancillary_in AS (
  SELECT
    pd.dasher_id,
    CAST(t.extract_datetime2 AS TIMESTAMP_NTZ) AS deposit_ts,
    ABS(t.transaction_amount_with_direction) AS amt
  FROM edw.payments.fact_crimson_transactions t
  JOIN payout_dasher pd ON t.payout_account_id = pd.payout_account_id
  WHERE t.txn_code_type IN ('IN_ATM','IN_AFT','IN_ACH','IN_OCT')
    AND t.extract_datetime2 IS NOT NULL
),
deposits_with_rank AS (
  SELECT
    a.dasher_id, a.deposit_ts, a.amt,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (PARTITION BY a.dasher_id ORDER BY a.deposit_ts) AS rn,
    SUM(a.amt)  OVER (PARTITION BY a.dasher_id) AS lifetime_anc
  FROM ancillary_in a
  JOIN joiners_in_window j ON j.dasher_id = a.dasher_id
  WHERE a.deposit_ts >= j.join_ts
),
deposit_summary AS (
  SELECT dasher_id,
    MAX(CASE WHEN rn = 1 THEN deposit_ts END) AS first_dep_ts,
    MAX(CASE WHEN rn = 2 THEN deposit_ts END) AS second_dep_ts,
    MAX(lifetime_anc)                          AS lifetime_anc
  FROM deposits_with_rank GROUP BY 1
)
SELECT
  COUNT(*)                                                           AS n_joiners,
  COUNT(ds.first_dep_ts)                                             AS n_1st,
  COUNT(ds.second_dep_ts)                                            AS n_2nd,
  SUM(CASE WHEN ds.lifetime_anc >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)            AS n_durable,
  SUM(CASE WHEN ds.second_dep_ts IS NOT NULL
            AND ds.lifetime_anc >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)            AS n_2nd_then_durable,
  SUM(CASE WHEN ds.first_dep_ts IS NOT NULL
            AND ds.second_dep_ts IS NULL
            AND ds.lifetime_anc >= 200 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)            AS n_1st_only_durable
FROM joiners_in_window jw
LEFT JOIN deposit_summary ds ON jw.dasher_id = ds.dasher_id;
