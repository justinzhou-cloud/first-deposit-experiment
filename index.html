Business problem

This experiment tests whether a bundled incentive — covering a user’s first purchase when they make an outside deposit — can activate the 89% of Crimson users who have never deposited. Depositors have 16× the POS spend and 50× the second-deposit rate of non-depositors. The offer incentivizes both deposit and spend in a single action, creating a first “money moment” that we believe drives sustained engagement. Stratifying by user tenure (F7D, F7–F28D, 28D+) tells us where on the user lifecycle this lever works best.

  • Card spend nearly doubles the day after deposit. OUT_POS volume is +98% in the 24h after vs. the 24h before the first deposit.
  • 67% of depositors swipe within 24h. Median time to first swipe: ~5 hours. Deposit and spend are one session, not two decisions.
  • Spending shifts toward need-based categories. Grocery share rises +2.8 pp (volume +149%). Rent, bills, and gaming rise. Gas declines. Deposits are pulled by intent to make a specific purchase.
  • The typical first deposit is small. Median: $21. 48% are under $20. This is a top-up to cover an immediate need, not “move my paycheck.”

3. Engagement ≠ durability

42–55% of new cohorts are engaged but not durable. Only ~1–2% reach durable. There are two parallel systems: an engagement system (dashing, spend) and an inflow system (external money in). Improving engagement does not automatically fix inflow.

What we should do

“Your first $10 purchase is on us when you make an outside deposit on Crimson.”

After the user makes an outside deposit ($10+) on Crimson, their first card purchase (any MCC, up to $10) is credited back automatically. One reward per user.

Why this framing

AlternativeProblem
Deposit-only bonus ($5 for first deposit)No reason to use the card afterward; doesn’t create a “money moment”
Spend-only bonus (cashback)Rewards behavior that already happens in 42–55% of engaged non-depositors; doesn’t move deposits
Two-step (deposit→spend→reward)Two drop-off points in a funnel where 94% don’t clear step 1
“First $10 purchase on us”Purchase is the headline; deposit is the natural action. One step, not two.

Hypotheses

  1. H1: The bundled offer breaks the $0 barrier. Treatment users will have a higher first-deposit rate than control.
  2. H2: Early users are more malleable. The deposit-rate lift will be largest for F7D, moderate for F7–F28D, and smallest for 28D+.
  3. H3: The first money moment drives spend. Treatment depositors will make their first card purchase faster (vs. ~5h organic median).
  4. H4: The behavior is sticky. Treatment depositors will show higher card spending and deposit activity in the 30 days after the credit vs. control.
  5. H5: Second deposit follows. Treatment depositors will deposit again within 30 days at a higher rate than the ~1.4% baseline.

Experiment design

MethodRandomized controlled trial, user-level, 50/50 split
EligibilityAll Crimson users who have never made an external deposit, regardless of tenure
StratificationOne treatment arm, one control. Results analyzed by tenure at enrollment:
• F7D (day 0–7): brand-new users
• F7–F28D (day 7–28): early users past first week
• 28D+: longer-tenured non-depositors
Tells us where on the lifecycle the incentive has the most impact.
Promo windowMay 1–15, 2026. Banner turns on at enrollment, off after 14 days or on redemption — whichever comes first. Last day to make a qualifying deposit: May 15, 2026.
Treatment“Your first $10 purchase is on us when you make an outside deposit on Crimson.” Single in-app banner. No drip sequence.
Qualifying depositAny outside deposit of $10+. Includes: direct deposit, other gig earnings, ACH, CashApp/Venmo/Apple Cash, cash. Excludes: DxPay, internal transfers, reversed/failed.
RewardCredit on first card purchase after qualifying deposit, up to $10. Spend $5 → get $5. Spend $10+ → get $10. One per user.

Why 14 days (May 1–15)

The banner turns on May 1 and turns off May 15 (or on redemption, whichever comes first).

ConsiderationRationale
Covers one full pay cycleMost gig workers are paid weekly or biweekly. 14 days ensures every user sees the offer during at least one payout when they have money to deposit.
Covers the high-leverage F7D windowThe first 7 days are the most malleable period for new users. 14 days fully includes this window plus a buffer for users whose spending need arises in week 2.
Conversion is fast once triggered67% of organic depositors swipe within 24h (median ~5h). The banner doesn’t need to stay up for weeks — it just needs to be present when the spending need hits.
Creates urgencyA 14-day window is short enough to motivate action. A 28-day window has no urgency and increases banner fatigue with no incremental signal.
7 days is too shortMisses users whose pay cycle or spending need falls in week 2. Also leaves no exposure window for F7–F28D users who enter the experiment mid-tenure.

Sizing & budget

The primary metric is first-deposit rate (baseline ~6%, detecting +3 pp lift). Sample size is set to power all three strata independently.

Value
Power analysisTwo-proportion z-test, 90% power, α=0.05: ~1,600 per arm for a +3 pp effect. Each of the three strata needs ≥1,600 per arm to be independently powered.
Target sample~5,000 per arm per stratum → 15,000 per arm (30,000 total). Provides comfortable buffer for attrition in each stratum.
Eligible poolAll non-depositor Crimson users. F7D is the smallest stratum (~7K new eligible/week); F7–F28D and 28D+ are much larger. Enrollment runs until F7D reaches target (~5 weeks).
Expected conversion~9% treatment vs. ~6% control
Max converting users~1,350 at $10 max each
Total budget cap~$14,000 in credits. Per-user cost fixed at $10 max. If conversion exceeds 12%, cap enrollment early.

What would tell us the experiment worked?

  1. Treatment has a statistically significant higher first-deposit rate than control.
  2. Treatment depositors become durable within 14 days at a higher rate than control depositors.
  3. In the 30 days after the credit (no incentive active), treatment depositors show higher card spending and deposit activity than control.

Decision framework

ResultWhat it meansNext step
All three strata show lift The incentive works regardless of tenure; the deposit barrier is spend-intent-driven across the lifecycle Scale to all non-depositors as a standing Crimson benefit

Only F7D shows lift The first week is the only malleable window Make it a sign-up bonus. Explore different levers for D7+ non-depositors.

Promotional Terms

To qualify for the purchase credit (the “Promotion”), participants must: (1) have an active DoorDash Crimson Account in good standing; (2) make a qualifying outside deposit of at least ten dollars ($10) into their DoorDash Crimson Deposit Account between May 1, 2026 and May 15, 2026; (3) make a card purchase after the qualifying deposit; and (4) not have previously redeemed this specific promotional reward. A qualifying outside deposit includes direct deposit (payroll from another employer), earnings from other gig platforms, ACH transfer from another bank account, transfers through CashApp, Apple Cash, or Venmo, or cash deposit at a participating retailer. Dashing earnings, internal transfers within Crimson, reversed deposits, and failed deposits do not qualify for this Promotion.

After completing a qualifying outside deposit, the participant’s first card purchase (any merchant category, up to $10) will be credited back to their DoorDash Crimson Account automatically. If the purchase amount is less than ten dollars ($10), the credit will equal the purchase amount. If the purchase is ten dollars ($10) or more, the credit will be ten dollars ($10).

This offer is available only to Dashers who received a direct communication about this promotion, cannot be transferred, and is not available to all Dashers. The last date to make a qualifying outside deposit under this promotion is May 15, 2026. The credit will be applied to the participant’s DoorDash Crimson Account within 10 business days after the qualifying purchase is completed.

General Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for this promotion?

Dashers who are eligible for this promotion must: (1) have an active DoorDash Crimson Account in good standing; (2) make a qualifying outside deposit of at least $10 into their DoorDash Crimson Deposit Account during the offer window; and (3) not have previously redeemed this specific promotional credit. This offer is not available to all Dashers. This offer is only available to Dashers who received a direct communication about this promotion and cannot be transferred.

What type of deposit qualifies for this promotion?

A qualifying outside deposit includes direct deposit (payroll from another employer), earnings from other gig platforms (Uber, Lyft, Instacart, etc.), ACH transfer from another bank account, transfers through CashApp, Apple Cash, or Venmo, or cash deposit at a participating retailer. Dashing earnings (DxPay), internal transfers within Crimson, reversed deposits, and failed deposits do not qualify.

Do earnings from other gig platforms qualify?

Yes, earnings from other gig platforms qualify as an outside deposit under this promotion.

How much do I get back?

After making a qualifying outside deposit of $10 or more, your first card purchase is covered up to $10. If you spend $5, you get $5 back. If you spend $10 or more, you get $10 back. Limit one credit per Dasher.

What can I buy with my first purchase?

Any purchase made with your DoorDash Crimson Visa Debit Card qualifies. There are no restrictions on merchant category — groceries, gas, bills, restaurants, online shopping, and anything else you use your card for.

When do I receive my credit?

Your credit will be applied directly to your DoorDash Crimson Account within 10 business days after your qualifying purchase is completed.

Do I need to deposit and purchase in the same day?

No. Once your qualifying outside deposit of $10 or more is received, your next card purchase (whenever you make it during the offer window) will be credited back up to $10.
