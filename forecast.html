The incentive isn't just accelerating recent joiners — it's unlocking dormant intent in long-tenured users who had settled into non-deposit behavior.

Implications for scaling

  • Don't exclude long-tenured users. 1Y+ users contribute the most incremental durables (137 of 357 total = 38%) despite having the lowest baseline. Removing them would sacrifice the largest segment of lift.
  • F30–F60D has the weakest relative lift (+5.4%) despite the highest baseline. These users are the most likely to deposit organically; the incentive adds less marginal value here. If budget is constrained, deprioritize this segment last — but it's still positive.
  • The forecast pool composition will shift over time. In month 1 the pool is dominated by 1Y+ and F180D–1Y users (existing stock). By month 4+, new inflow (F30–F60D) becomes a larger share. Expect the blended lift to start high and gradually decline toward the F30–F60D rate.
6 Risks and caveats
  • Lift attenuation at scale: Exp 2 ran for 22 days on a pre-screened cohort. An always-on program cycles through the highest-intent users first; later months pull from a lower-intent pool. Expect lift to decay 25–50% over time.
  • Saturation: The eligible pool grows ~13K/month (net of conversions). Within 3–4 months, the program will have touched most of the current high-trust pool. Growth becomes dependent on new joiner inflow.
  • Quality discount: Exp 2 treatment durables were ~15% lower quality than organic ones. The forecast applies an 85% quality factor, but this could worsen at scale as the marginal user gets progressively less engaged.
  • Cannibalization: Some organic converters (the 38K "deadweight") may delay their deposit to ensure they qualify for the bonus. This doesn't create new cost but does shift timing.
  • This does not account for LTV. The impact estimates (POS spend, dashing hours) are activity levels, not profit. The ROI case requires a durability LTV model to compare the $886K spend against lifetime value of ~5.3K incremental durables.
7 Bottom line

At Exp 2's measured rates and $20 reward:

  • ~6,262 incremental durables through EOY (~5,323 quality-adjusted)
  • ~$886K total incentive spend
  • ~$141/durable raw, ~$167/durable quality-adjusted

Both numbers beat the brief's $200–$250 evergreen threshold. If your $10-on-us experiment achieves comparable lift at half the payout, the same 8-month forecast drops to ~$443K total spend at ~$71/durable raw — a strong evergreen case.

The biggest risk is lift attenuation. If lift drops to 50% of Exp 2 levels at scale, cost rises to $283/durable — still within range but no longer a clear win. The experiment you're about to launch will tell us whether the bundled mechanism holds up at a lower reward.
